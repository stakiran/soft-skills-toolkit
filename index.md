# Communication

## > Conversations and Meetings
- MAMA (Meeting As An AMA): [JA](mama_meeting_as_an_ama.md) [EN](mama_meeting_as_an_ama_EN.md)
    - T: Managers who want to draw out more input from team members
    - E: In a single 30-minute meeting, you can answer 20+ questions
    - A: Run meetings strictly as an AMA
- RAMA (Realtime AMA): [JA](rama_realtime_ama.md) [EN](rama_realtime_ama_EN.md)
    - T: Managers who want to draw out more input from team members
    - E: More than double the number of member comments; since you also answer questions, you can improve the meeting experience as well
    - A: Add an AMA segment to the meeting agenda
- RMA (Request Me Anything): [JA](rma_request_me_anything.md) [EN](rma_request_me_anything_EN.md)
    - T: Managers who are in a position to receive approval requests and other asks
    - E: Minimize the overhead of handling each individual request
    - A: Optimize your response per typical request pattern (Five Category)

# Documentation
- The Simplate Principle: [JA](simplate_simple_template_principle.md) [EN](simplate_simple_template_principle_EN.md)
    - T: People who create and distribute templates, especially those in a position to apply them to a team
    - E: Reduce the cost of creating, sharing, and using templates to one-third of the previous level (here, cost means time)
    - A: Keep the template to a single page, and make it easy to scan by adding categories and whitespace
- TEA line for writing summaries: [JA](summary_line_TEA.md) [EN](summary_line_TEA_EN.md)
    - T: Anyone who writes business or technical documents/articles
    - E: Reduce readers' time cost to judge the value to one-quarter
    - A: Write the summary in three lines: Target, Effect, Action
