- How to read the TEA line
    - T (Target): The primary audience for this tool
    - E (Effect): The benefits the audience will get
    - A (Action): The approach this tool takes: the mindset and/or practices to be installed in the audience

# Communication

## > Conversations and Meetings
- MAMA (Meeting As An AMA): [JA](mama_meeting_as_an_ama.md) [EN](mama_meeting_as_an_ama_EN.md)
    - T: Managers who want to draw out more input from their members
    - E: You can answer 20+ questions in a single 30-minute meeting
    - A: Run meetings strictly as an AMA
- RAMA (Realtime AMA): [JA](rama_realtime_ama.md) [EN](rama_realtime_ama_EN.md)
    - T: Managers who want to draw out more input from their members
    - E: Increase the number of member contributions to at least 2x the usual level, and improve the meeting experience by answering questions
    - A: Add an AMA segment to the meeting agenda
- RMA (Request Me Anything): [JA](rma_request_me_anything.md) [EN](rma_request_me_anything_EN.md)
    - T: Managers who are in a position to receive approval requests and other asks
    - E: Minimize the overhead per request
    - A: Optimize how you handle each typical request pattern (Five Category)
- Manager As A Function: [JA](manager_as_a_function.md) [EN](manager_as_a_function_EN.md)
    - T: Managers who feel they are a bottleneck, or members who believe their manager is a bottleneck
    - E: Improve managers' task throughput efficiency by multiple times
    - A: Managers treat themselves as a function, provide an interface, and have members use it

# Documentation

## > Value Proposition
- TEA line for writing summaries: [JA](summary_line_TEA.md) [EN](summary_line_TEA_EN.md)
    - T: Anyone who writes business or technical documents/articles
    - E: Cut the reader's time cost to assess value to 1/4
    - A: Write the summary in three lines: Target, Effect, Action
- Proposal Triad: [JA](proposal_triad.md) [EN](proposal_triad_EN.md)
    - T: Anyone who makes proposals
    - E: Cut the rate at which proposals get ignored to 1/2
    - A: Use it as a reference because the recommended check items for proposal review (or before executing a proposal) are summarized in three lines

# Agile Work
- Casual Agile: [JA](casual_ajile.md) [EN](casual_ajile_EN.md)
    - T: Managers and executives who are used to planned, control-oriented ways of working and can't move quickly
    - E: Reduce labor costs for management and communication to 1/10
    - A: Work exploratorily by selectively using "small tools" that incorporate the essence of Agile
- Personal Daily Workspace (PDW): [JA](personal_daily_workspace.md) [EN](personal_daily_workspace_EN.md)
    - T: Anyone whose work has become ad hoc, can't reflect effectively, and feels stuck
    - E: Instead of spending 30 minutes every day, you can record your work and thinking daily and be able to reflect
    - A: Create a personal note space per day and live in it as your home base

# Creative Thinking
- Divergent work using GitHub: [JA](divergent_work_using_github.md) [EN](divergent_work_using_github_EN.md)
    - T: Teams that want to do collaborative creative thinking on GitHub + plain text
    - E: A team of six can produce 100,000 characters' worth of ideas and discussion on a single theme, asynchronously, in one week
    - A: Create a repository, and have each person write according to `(Theme).md` and `(Theme)-(MemberName).md`. Also push and pull as needed

# Meta Soft Skills

## > Soft Skills Engineering
- Seven Lists principles for turning soft skills into tools: [JA](seven_lists_for_soft_skills.md) [EN](seven_lists_for_soft_skills_EN.md)
    - T: People who want to advocate for the importance of soft skills, or engineers/managers who want to become proficient in them
    - E: Reduce the thinking cost when creating tools. Be able to decide direction in one minute
    - A: Since there are seven perspectives, choose which one you want to create
- Simplate principle: [JA](simplate_simple_template_principle.md) [EN](simplate_simple_template_principle_EN.md)
    - T: People who create and distribute templates, especially those in a position to apply them to a team
    - E: Reduce the cost of creating, sharing, and using templates to 1/3 (cost here means time)
    - A: Fit the template on a single page and make it easy to read by adding categories and whitespace
- SESSEI (Subjective Effect of Soft Skills Engineering Index): [JA](sessei_subjective_effect_of_soft_skills_engineering_index.md) [EN](sessei_subjective_effect_of_soft_skills_engineering_index_EN.md)
    - T: People engaged in "hard-to-measure work" such as developing, promoting, and advocating soft skills
    - E: Make it measurable, and be able to persuade people who won't move without metrics
    - A: Have people answer a simple, easy-to-respond-to survey with seven questions
