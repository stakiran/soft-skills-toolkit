- How to read the TEA line
    - T (Target): The primary audience for this tool
    - E (Effect): The effect the audience will gain
    - A (Action): The approach this tool takes: the mindset and practices that will be installed in the audience

# Communication

## > Asynchronous communication
- Relay-style communication: [JA](relaying.md) [EN](relaying_EN.md)
    - T: Managers and drivers who are struggling with members who don't follow requests
    - E: The follow-up effort for people who don't comply becomes "one person at a time," reducing follow-up costs
    - A: Have people take turns one by one, like passing a baton

## > Conversations and meetings
- MAMA (Meeting As An AMA): [JA](mama_meeting_as_an_ama.md) [EN](mama_meeting_as_an_ama_EN.md)
    - T: Managers who want to elicit more input from members
    - E: You can answer 20+ questions in a single 30-minute meeting
    - A: Run a meeting dedicated to being an AMA
- RAMA (Realtime AMA): [JA](rama_realtime_ama.md) [EN](rama_realtime_ama_EN.md)
    - T: Managers who want to elicit more input from members
    - E: Increase member speaking turns to 2x or more than before, and improve the meeting experience by answering questions
    - A: Add an AMA segment to the meeting agenda
- RMA (Request Me Anything): [JA](rma_request_me_anything.md) [EN](rma_request_me_anything_EN.md)
    - T: Managers who are in the position of receiving requests such as approvals
    - E: Minimize the overhead per request
    - A: Optimize responses by typical request patterns (Five Category)
- Manager as a Function: [JA](manager_as_a_function.md) [EN](manager_as_a_function_EN.md)
    - T: Managers who feel they are the bottleneck, or members who think the manager is the bottleneck
    - E: Improve the manager's task processing efficiency by several times or more
    - A: The manager treats themself as a function, provides interfaces, and has members use them

# Documentation

## > Positioning
- TEA line for writing summaries: [JA](summary_line_TEA.md) [EN](summary_line_TEA_EN.md)
    - T: Anyone who writes business or technical documents/articles
    - E: Reduce the reader's time cost to judge value to 1/4
    - A: Write a summary in three lines: Target, Effect, Action
- Proposal Triad: [JA](proposal_triad.md) [EN](proposal_triad_EN.md)
    - T: Anyone who makes proposals
    - E: Cut the rate of proposals being ignored in half
    - A: Use it as a reference because the recommended checkpoints—when evaluating a proposal or before executing it—are summarized in three lines

# Agile Work
- Casual Agile: [JA](casual_ajile.md) [EN](casual_ajile_EN.md)
    - T: Managers and executives who are accustomed to planned/controlled ways of working and can't move quickly
    - E: Reduce labor costs for management and communication to 1/10
    - A: Work exploratorily by selectively using "small tools" that incorporate the essence of agile
- Personal Daily Workspace (PDW): [JA](personal_daily_workspace.md) [EN](personal_daily_workspace_EN.md)
    - T: Anyone whose work has become ad hoc, who can't reflect, and feels stuck
    - E: By spending 30 minutes each day, you can record your work and thinking every day and become able to reflect
    - A: Create a daily memo space for yourself and live out of it as your home
- Casual Innovation: [JA](casual_innovation.md) [EN](casual_innovation_EN.md)
    - T: Managers, executives, and chiefs who want innovation but don't know how to leverage talent
    - E: Increase the number of hypothesis validations for innovative ideas by 5x or more
    - A: Define the requirements for individuals to focus alone, and have those conditions secured accordingly

# Creative Thinking
- Divergent work using GitHub: [JA](divergent_work_using_github.md) [EN](divergent_work_using_github_EN.md)
    - T: Teams that want to do collaborative creative thinking using GitHub + plain text
    - E: A team of six can asynchronously produce 100,000 characters worth of ideas and discussion on a single theme in one week
    - A: Create one repository, and have each person write following `(Theme).md` and `(Theme)-(MemberName).md`. Also do pushes and pulls

# Meta Soft Skills

## > Soft Skills Engineering
- The Seven Lists principle for turning soft skills into tools: [JA](seven_lists_for_soft_skills.md) [EN](seven_lists_for_soft_skills_EN.md)
    - T: People who want to advocate the importance of soft skills, or engineers/managers who want to be able to use them effectively
    - E: Reduce the thinking cost of creating tools. You'll be able to judge direction in one minute
    - A: There are seven perspectives, so choose which one you want to create
- Simplate principle: [JA](simplate_simple_template_principle.md) [EN](simplate_simple_template_principle_EN.md)
    - T: People who create and distribute templates, especially those in a position to apply them to a team
    - E: Reduce the cost of creating, sharing, and using templates to 1/3 of the previous level (here, cost means time)
    - A: Keep the template to a single page and make it easier to read by adding categories and whitespace
- SESSEI (Subjective Effect of Soft Skills Engineering Index): [JA](sessei_subjective_effect_of_soft_skills_engineering_index.md) [EN](sessei_subjective_effect_of_soft_skills_engineering_index_EN.md)
    - T: People engaged in "hard-to-measure work" such as developing, driving, and advocating soft skills
    - E: Make it measurable, enabling you to persuade people who won't act without metrics
    - A: Have people answer a simple, easy-to-respond-to survey with seven questions
