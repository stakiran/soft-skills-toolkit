- How to read the TEA line
    - T (Target): The primary audience for this tool
    - E (Effect): The benefits the audience will gain
    - A (Action): The approach this tool takes: the mindset and practices installed in the audience

# Communication

## > Conversations and meetings
- MAMA (Meeting As An AMA): [JA](mama_meeting_as_an_ama.md) [EN](mama_meeting_as_an_ama_EN.md)
    - T: Managers who want to draw out more input from team members
    - E: In a single 30-minute meeting, you can answer 20+ questions
    - A: Run meetings strictly as AMAs
- RAMA (Realtime AMA): [JA](rama_realtime_ama.md) [EN](rama_realtime_ama_EN.md)
    - T: Managers who want to draw out more input from team members
    - E: Increase the number of member contributions to more than 2x the usual level, and improve the meeting experience by answering questions
    - A: Add an AMA segment to the meeting agenda
- RMA (Request Me Anything): [JA](rma_request_me_anything.md) [EN](rma_request_me_anything_EN.md)
    - T: Managers in positions where they receive approvals and other requests
    - E: Minimize the overhead per request
    - A: Optimize your handling by typical request pattern (Five Category)
- Manager As A Function: [JA](manager_as_a_function.md) [EN](manager_as_a_function_EN.md)
    - T: Managers who feel they are the bottleneck, or team members who believe the manager is the bottleneck
    - E: Improve the manager's task throughput by several times or more
    - A: The manager treats themselves as a function, provides an interface, and has team members "use" it

# Documentation
- The Simplate Principle: [JA](simplate_simple_template_principle.md) [EN](simplate_simple_template_principle_EN.md)
    - T: People who create and distribute templates, especially those applying them to a team
    - E: Reduce the time cost of creating, sharing, and using templates to 1/3 of the previous level (here, cost means time)
    - A: Keep each template to a single page, and make it easy to read by adding categories and whitespace
- TEA line for writing summaries: [JA](summary_line_TEA.md) [EN](summary_line_TEA_EN.md)
    - T: Anyone who writes business or technical documents/articles
    - E: Reduce the reader's time cost to judge value to 1/4
    - A: Write summaries in three lines: Target, Effect, Action

# Agile Work
- Casual Agile: [JA](casual_ajile.md) [EN](casual_ajile_EN.md)
    - T: Managers and executives who are accustomed to planned, control-oriented ways of working and can't move quickly
    - E: Reduce labor costs for management and communication to 1/10
    - A: Work exploratorily by selecting and combining "small tools" that incorporate the essence of agile
- Personal Daily Workspace (PDW): [JA](personal_daily_workspace.md) [EN](personal_daily_workspace_EN.md)
    - T: Anyone whose work has become ad hoc, who can't reflect, and who feels stuck
    - E: By spending 30 minutes per day, you can record your work and thinking every day and become able to reflect
    - A: Create a personal note space for each day, and live out of it as your home base

# Creative Thinking
- Divergent work using GitHub: [JA](divergent_work_using_github.md) [EN](divergent_work_using_github_EN.md)
    - T: Teams that want to do creative thinking collaboratively using GitHub + plain text
    - E: A team of six can produce 100,000 characters worth of ideas and discussion on a single theme asynchronously in one week
    - A: Create one repository, and have each person write following `(Theme).md` and `(Theme)-(MemberName).md`. Also push and pull

# Meta Soft Skills

## > Soft skills engineering
- The Seven Lists principle for turning soft skills into tools: [JA](seven_lists_for_soft_skills.md) [EN](seven_lists_for_soft_skills_EN.md)
    - T: People who want to advocate the importance of soft skills, or engineers/managers who want to become proficient at them
    - E: Reduce the thinking cost when creating tools; you can decide direction in 1 minute
    - A: Since there are seven perspectives, choose which one you want to create
- SESSEI (Subjective Effect of Soft Skills Engineering Index): [JA](sessei_subjective_effect_of_soft_skills_engineering_index.md) [EN](sessei_subjective_effect_of_soft_skills_engineering_index_EN.md)
    - T: People engaged in "hard-to-measure work" such as developing, driving, and advocating soft skills
    - E: Make it measurable, enabling you to persuade people who won't move without metrics
    - A: Have people answer a simple, easy-to-answer survey with seven questions
