- How to read a TEA line
    - T (Target): The tool's primary audience
    - E (Effect): The effect the audience can gain
    - A (Action): The approach this tool adopts: the mindset and practices that will be "installed" in the audience

# Communication

## > Asynchronous Communication
- Relaying-style communication: [JA](relaying.md) [EN](relaying_EN.md)
    - T: Managers and drivers who are troubled by members who don't follow through on requests
    - E: Follow-up overhead for people who don't comply becomes "one at a time," reducing follow-up cost
    - A: Make it go around one person at a time, like passing a baton

## > Conversations and Meetings
- MAMA (Meeting As An AMA): [JA](mama_meeting_as_an_ama.md) [EN](mama_meeting_as_an_ama_EN.md)
    - T: Managers who want to draw more input from members
    - E: In a single 30-minute meeting, you can answer 20+ questions
    - A: Run a meeting devoted entirely to AMA
- RAMA (Realtime AMA): [JA](rama_realtime_ama.md) [EN](rama_realtime_ama_EN.md)
    - T: Managers who want to draw more input from members
    - E: More than double the number of member contributions, and also improve the meeting experience by answering questions
    - A: Add an AMA segment to the meeting agenda
- RMA (Request Me Anything): [JA](rma_request_me_anything.md) [EN](rma_request_me_anything_EN.md)
    - T: Managers who are in a position to receive approvals and other requests
    - E: Minimize the overhead per request
    - A: Optimize your handling per typical request pattern (Five Category)
- Manager as a function (Manager As A Function): [JA](manager_as_a_function.md) [EN](manager_as_a_function_EN.md)
    - T: Managers who feel they are a bottleneck, or members who believe their manager is a bottleneck
    - E: Increase the manager's task-processing efficiency by multiple times
    - A: The manager treats themselves as a function, provides an interface, and has members use it

# Documentation

## > Positioning
- TEA lines for writing summaries: [JA](summary_line_TEA.md) [EN](summary_line_TEA_EN.md)
    - T: Anyone who writes business or technical documents/articles
    - E: Reduce the time cost for readers to judge value to 1/4
    - A: Write a summary in three lines: Target, Effect, Action
- Proposal Triad: [JA](proposal_triad.md) [EN](proposal_triad_EN.md)
    - T: Anyone who makes proposals
    - E: Cut the rate at which proposals get ignored in half
    - A: Use it as a reference, since the recommended confirmation items (during proposal review or before executing a proposal) are summarized in three lines

# Agile Work
- Casual Agile: [JA](casual_ajile.md) [EN](casual_ajile_EN.md)
    - T: Managers and executives who are used to planned, management-heavy ways of working and can't move quickly
    - E: Reduce labor costs for management and communication to 1/10
    - A: Work exploratorily by selectively applying "small tools" that incorporate the essence of Agile
- Personal Daily Workspace (PDW): [JA](personal_daily_workspace.md) [EN](personal_daily_workspace_EN.md)
    - T: Anyone whose work has become ad hoc, who can't reflect, and feels stuck
    - E: Instead of spending 30 minutes every day, you can record your work and thinking daily and be able to reflect
    - A: Create a personal memo space for each day and use it as your home base

# Creative Thinking
- Divergent work using GitHub: [JA](divergent_work_using_github.md) [EN](divergent_work_using_github_EN.md)
    - T: Teams that want to do collaborative creative thinking using GitHub + plain text
    - E: A 6-person team can produce 100,000 characters worth of ideas and discussion asynchronously in a week on a single theme
    - A: Create one repository, and have everyone write following `(Theme).md` and `(Theme)-(MemberName).md`. Also do pushes and pulls

# Meta Soft Skills

## > Soft Skills Engineering
- The Seven Lists principle for turning soft skills into tools: [JA](seven_lists_for_soft_skills.md) [EN](seven_lists_for_soft_skills_EN.md)
    - T: People who want to evangelize the importance of soft skills, or engineers and managers who want to become proficient at them
    - E: Reduce thinking cost when creating tools; be able to decide a direction in 1 minute
    - A: Since there are seven viewpoints, choose which one you want to create
- Simplate principle: [JA](simplate_simple_template_principle.md) [EN](simplate_simple_template_principle_EN.md)
    - T: People who create and distribute templates, especially those in a position to apply them to a team
    - E: Reduce the cost (time) to create, share, and use templates to 1/3 of what it used to be
    - A: Keep a template to a single page, and make it easy to read by adding categories and whitespace
- SESSEI (Subjective Effect of Soft Skills Engineering Index): [JA](sessei_subjective_effect_of_soft_skills_engineering_index.md) [EN](sessei_subjective_effect_of_soft_skills_engineering_index_EN.md)
    - T: People engaged in "hard-to-measure work" such as developing, driving, or evangelizing soft skills
    - E: Make it measurable, enabling you to persuade people who won't act without metrics
    - A: Have people answer a survey of seven simple, easy-to-answer questions
